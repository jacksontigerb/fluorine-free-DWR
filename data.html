The lab notebook

Methods and evidence behind the headline story, updated as the project runs. Plain figures, honest caveats.

Methods at a glance

Substrate

Scoured woven cotton. Sacrificial swatches confirm the cure conditions; fabrics supplied by Finisterre carry the core study.

Coating route

A fluorine-free waterborne dispersion of anatase TiO₂ nanoparticles in a siloxane-stearyl acrylate system, applied to cotton by dip-pad-cure. Wet pickup and dry add-on are logged for every swatch.

Cure

An oven cure forms the network and bonds it to the fibre, so the texture and low-energy layer survive handling rather than rinsing away. Oven temperature is measured, not assumed, and bath age is logged.

Measurement

Water contact angle, hysteresis and roll-off for wetting; abrasion for durability; SEM for morphology, XPS and ATR-FTIR for surface chemistry, XRD for the anatase phase, and TGA for composition. Three or more swatches per condition.

Confirming what is on the fibre

The coating only counts if the right chemistry sits at the surface and no fluorine is doing the work. ATR-FTIR identifies the organic groups of the siloxane-stearyl acrylate network, the long C-H stretches of the stearyl chains and the Si-O-Si backbone, while XPS reads the outermost surface elements directly, confirming silicon and titanium are present and fluorine is absent. XRD checks that the TiO₂ is in the anatase phase.

Caveats, because they matter: band assignments are being cross-checked against uncoated controls, peak intensities are not yet quantified, and surface-sensitive XPS is read alongside the bulk methods rather than on its own.
