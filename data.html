The lab notebook

Methods and evidence behind the headline story, updated as the project runs. Plain figures, honest caveats.

Methods at a glance

Substrate

Scoured woven cotton. Sacrificial swatches confirm the cure conditions; fabrics supplied by Finisterre carry the core study.

Coating route

VTMS-functionalised TiO₂ grafted to the fibre first, then a fluorine-free waterborne emulsion with a blocked isocyanate crosslinker, applied by padding. Wet pickup and dry add-on are logged for every swatch.

Cure

Oven cure at a working setpoint of 130 °C for 3 to 5 minutes. Oven temperature is measured, not assumed, and bath age is logged.

Measurement

ATR-FTIR for surface chemistry, SEM and EDX for morphology, TGA for composition, profilometry for film build, and water contact angle for the headline result. Three or more swatches per condition.

Catching the crosslinker unlock

The blocked isocyanate only does its job if heat really removes the caps. Films cured at 100, 110 and 130 °C were compared by ATR-FTIR against an uncured baseline. After local baseline correction, a weak but real free-NCO band appears at 2278 cm⁻¹, and only in the film cured at 130 °C. The supplier's DSC data put the deblocking onset at 99 °C, so a 130 °C cure carries roughly 30 degrees of kinetic margin.

Caveats, because they matter: a single film per condition so far, the band is detected rather than quantified, and the 130 °C film also had a longer dwell, so temperature and time are not yet separated. The confirmation on actual cotton, where regenerated NCO is trapped as urethane, is the next experiment.
