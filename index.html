Can cotton repel water without the forever chemicals?

A masters research project testing whether anatase TiO₂ nanoparticles, dispersed in a siloxane-stearyl acrylate network and padded onto ordinary cotton, can make water bead and roll away with no fluorine involved.

01 · Why fluorine-free

For decades, staying dry meant forever chemicals.

Durable water repellency, the finish that makes rain bead off a jacket, was built for half a century on fluorocarbons. Their CF₃ tips give the lowest surface energy known, so they shed both water and oil. The trouble is that the carbon-to-fluorine bond barely breaks down in nature. These are the PFAS, the “forever chemicals,” and regulators are now phasing them out of textiles.

The performance was never the problem. The persistence was.

So the research question is simple to state. Can a coating match that water repellency, survive real use, and leave nothing behind that lasts for centuries? This project tests one route to a yes. It is built on two ideas that are ordinary on their own, but useful once combined and bonded onto cotton, with no fluorinated chains anywhere.

02 · The concept

Two components decide how a drop behaves: roughness and surface energy.

Whether water spreads or beads comes down to the contact angle. Lower the surface energy and add roughness at the right scale, and a droplet can move from wetting the fabric to riding on a cushion of trapped air.

03 · Chemistry & materials

One coating, doing both jobs at once.

The anatase TiO₂ nanoparticles supply the roughness. The siloxane-stearyl acrylate network supplies the low energy skin and binds the whole thing to the fibre.

Why these ingredients. Anatase is the well characterised, photoactive form of TiO₂ and gives controllable nanoparticle roughness. The siloxane segments contribute a flexible, naturally low energy backbone. The long stearyl side chains pack into a hydrophobic outer layer. The acrylate chemistry crosslinks it all into a continuous network that grips the cellulose surface. No fluorine is involved at any step.

04 · Fabrication

Three steps: dip, pad, cure.

A textile scale route. It uses the same dip, pad and cure sequence as industrial finishing, so the chemistry has a credible path out of the lab.

Step 1, Dip

Immerse in the dispersion

Cotton is submerged in a waterborne dispersion of anatase TiO₂ and the siloxane-stearyl acrylate components, so the chemistry reaches into the weave and onto every fibre surface.

Step 2, Pad

Squeeze to an even pickup

Passing through nip rollers sets a uniform, controlled wet pickup. This is what keeps the coating reproducible rather than blotchy, and it is the step that scales to a finishing line.

Step 3, Cure

Heat to lock it on

A thermal cure drives off the water and forms the network, crosslinking the matrix and bonding it to the fibre, so the texture and the low energy skin survive handling and washing rather than rinsing away.

05 · Characterisation

How the coating is interrogated.

Performance is measured directly. Structure and chemistry are pinned down with a standard materials toolkit. Each method answers one question.

Wetting

Water contact angle

The headline number: how spherical a static droplet sits. Higher means more repellent.

Wetting

Contact-angle hysteresis

The gap between advancing and receding angles. Low hysteresis means a drop barely pins to the surface.

Wetting

Roll-off angle

The tilt at which a drop slides off. A low roll-off angle is the practical sign of self-cleaning behaviour.

Durability

Abrasion

Mechanical rubbing to probe whether repellency survives wear, the real test of a bonded coating against a loose film.

Morphology

SEM

Electron microscopy images the hierarchical roughness the nanoparticles build on each fibre.

Surface chemistry

XPS

X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy reads the elements at the outermost surface, confirming Si, Ti and the absence of fluorine.

Bonding

ATR-FTIR

Infrared spectra identify the chemical groups present and track how the network forms on curing.

Crystallinity

XRD

X-ray diffraction confirms the TiO₂ is the anatase phase and gauges particle crystallinity.

Thermal

TGA

Thermogravimetry quantifies organic content and the coating’s thermal stability by mass loss on heating.

07 · Significance

A coating that performs, then disappears responsibly.

If a fluorine-free finish can match PFAS repellency and survive real wear, the case for forever chemicals in textiles gets much weaker. That is the wider stake here: high performance technical textiles that do not outlast the planet.

The next phase puts the chemistry on real outdoor fabrics from Finisterre, the Cornish brand, with the full characterisation set applied to each. The science is being kept in the open, methods, spectra and honest caveats included.
