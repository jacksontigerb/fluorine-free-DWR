Working with Finisterre

Alongside the core project, I worked with Finisterre, the Cornish outdoor brand, to characterise a set of their fabrics. This page is what I set out to measure, and what I have found so far.

Where things stand. The fabrics are in. Baseline characterisation comes first, so every later comparison has an honest starting point, then the coated comparisons follow. The results below fill in as I run them, so treat anything unmarked as still in progress.

last updated 14 June 2026

What I measured

Five views of the same question: how does this fabric handle water, and what is its surface really like?

Water contact angle and roll-off

The numbers behind “rain rolls off”. I photograph a droplet on the surface and measure its angle, and the tilt at which it slides away, before coating, after coating, and after washing. The target is a high contact angle with a low roll-off, held after washing.

Surface chemistry

I use ATR-FTIR and XPS to read which chemical groups and elements sit at the surface, and to confirm there is no fluorine doing the work.

Fibre morphology

SEM shows the weave and fibre surface up close: texture, how evenly the coating covers, and how the finish changes the fibre.

Thermal profile

TGA weighs the fabric as it heats, separating fibre from finish and quantifying how much coating is really there.

Wash durability

Repellency that disappears in the first wash is not repellency. Coated swatches go through wash cycles and I re-measure each time.
