What gets measured

Five views of the same question: how does this fabric handle water, and what is its surface really like?

Water contact angle and beading

The number behind “rain rolls off”. A droplet is photographed on the surface and its angle measured, before coating, after coating, and after washing — alongside how easily it rolls off when the fabric is tilted. The target is a superhydrophobic surface: a high contact angle and a low roll-off, held after washing.

Surface chemistry

Infrared spectroscopy (ATR-FTIR) and XPS read which chemical groups and elements sit at the surface, and confirm there is no fluorine doing the work — the repellency comes from anatase TiO₂ texture and a siloxane–stearyl layer.

Fibre morphology

Electron microscopy (SEM) shows the weave and fibre surface up close: texture, coating coverage, and how the finish changes the fibre.

Thermal profile

Thermogravimetric analysis (TGA) weighs the fabric as it heats, separating fibre from finish and quantifying how much coating is really there.

Wash durability

Repellency that disappears in the first wash is not repellency. Coated swatches go through wash cycles and get re-measured each time.

What comes back to you

  • A one-page summary. The findings in plain English, written for product and materials people rather than examiners.
  • The full dataset on request. Spectra, micrographs and contact-angle statistics, with methods.
  • This page, kept current. Results land here as they are measured, so there is always one link with the latest picture.
